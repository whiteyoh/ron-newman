# Safe Use in Schools

Glytch is designed for learning. It is a simulated, workshop-safe demo.

It does not replace school policy, safeguarding judgement, data protection practice, or professional review.

## Core rule

Do not enter personal pupil data or sensitive school data into the demo.

Use generic examples only.

## What not to enter

Do not enter:

- pupil names
- parent or carer details
- addresses
- phone numbers
- email addresses
- medical information
- behaviour records
- safeguarding concerns
- assessment data linked to a real pupil
- staff HR information
- confidential school documents
- anything that would identify a pupil or family

## What is safe to use

Use generic, fictional or teacher-created examples such as:

- a revision timetable for a fictional student
- a generic paragraph that needs improving
- teacher-provided notes with no personal information
- a made-up careers research task
- a generic debate topic
- a fictional school club planning example

## Safe workshop behaviour

Before running a session, remind the group:

- AI answers can be wrong.
- AI answers can sound confident and still be inaccurate.
- People must check before using outputs.
- Evidence matters.
- The final decision stays with a person.
- AI should support learning, not replace thinking.

## What Glytch does safely

Glytch is workshop-safe because:

- runs are simulated
- outputs are for learning
- no real emails are sent
- no files are changed
- no real-world actions are taken
- people can compare levels without handing control to AI
- review points are part of the learning model

## What Glytch does not guarantee

Glytch does not guarantee:

- legal compliance
- safeguarding compliance
- accuracy of every AI output
- suitability for every school policy
- that AI should be used for every task

Schools should follow their own policies and relevant safeguarding, data protection and AI guidance.

## Good safety questions

Ask these during the workshop:

- What information did the AI use?
- Could any of that information identify a real person?
- What could be wrong?
- What evidence would we need?
- Who should check this before use?
- Is AI appropriate for this task?
- Would using AI help learning, or replace learning?

## Simple message for students

AI can help you think, plan and check.

It should not replace your thinking.

Do not use private information.

Do not trust an answer just because it sounds confident.

Check before you use it.
