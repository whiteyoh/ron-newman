# Glytch One-Page Brief

![Glytch banner](assets/glytch-readme-banner.png)

Helping people understand AI beyond the chat box.

## What Glytch is

Glytch is a workshop-safe AI fluency tool that helps people understand the difference between a quick AI answer and a checked, reviewable AI-supported workflow.

It teaches AI judgement, not just AI prompts.

## The problem it solves

People are using AI before they fully understand how to check it.

A normal AI tool can produce an answer quickly, but users may not know:

- what the AI assumed
- what evidence was used
- what still needs checking
- where human review belongs
- when a simple prompt is enough
- when a more controlled workflow is safer

Glytch makes those hidden parts visible.

## Why this matters now

AI tools are becoming easier to access and harder to avoid.

Schools, teams and organisations need people to build practical judgement before they are asked to trust AI-supported outputs or automate real work.

Glytch gives people a safe way to explore that judgement without taking real-world action.

## What the demo shows

Glytch lets people compare all eight levels of AI support:

| Level | What it shows |
|---:|---|
| 1 | A quick AI answer |
| 2 | Clearer instructions, format and constraints |
| 3 | A bounded tool-style path |
| 4 | A more evidence-aware answer |
| 5 | A visible plan before action |
| 6 | Critique and revision before use |
| 7 | A controlled loop with stop conditions |
| 8 | Simulated orchestration with review gates |

The core lesson is:

> Use the lowest level of AI support that safely gets the job done.

## Why it is safe

Glytch is designed for workshop learning.

It does not:

- take real-world action
- send messages
- change files
- make decisions for people
- replace human judgement
- act as a production automation platform
- act as a fact-checking engine

The demo keeps the focus on structure, evidence, checks and human review.

## How we would pilot it

Run a small pilot with one school, team or workshop group.

Recommended first pilot:

- 8 to 20 people
- 45 to 60 minutes
- safe, generic scenarios only
- before-and-after confidence questions
- short feedback capture
- no personal or sensitive data

Use the [Pilot plan](PILOT_PLAN.md) to run the session and collect evidence.

## What evidence we would collect

The pilot would measure whether people leave with a clearer understanding of:

- what the AI did
- what evidence was used
- what needs checking
- where human review belongs
- why more automation is not automatically better

Suggested evidence:

- before-and-after confidence scores
- participant comments
- facilitator observations
- examples of changed understanding
- risks or concerns raised
- recommendation to continue, revise, stop or expand

## The ask

Approve a small pilot with one school, team or workshop group to test whether Glytch improves practical AI judgement.

The pilot should be safe, measurable and limited in scope.

## One-line summary

Glytch is a safe, practical way to help people understand AI before they are asked to trust it.
