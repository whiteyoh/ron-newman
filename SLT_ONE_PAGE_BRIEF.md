# Glytch: One-Page Brief for School Leaders

## What is Glytch?

Glytch is a workshop-safe AI fluency demo for schools.

It helps staff and students understand how AI use moves from simple chat into checked, evidence-aware and reviewable work.

The aim is not to replace judgement. The aim is to help people understand where judgement, evidence and human review still matter.

## Why it matters

Schools need practical AI literacy that goes beyond prompt-writing.

Students and staff need to know:

- how to ask better questions
- how to check AI outputs
- why evidence matters
- where AI can help learning
- where AI can harm learning
- when a person must stay in control
- why more automation is not always better

## Link to wider AI literacy themes

Glytch is not affiliated with UNESCO and is not endorsed by UNESCO.

It is designed to support the same broad AI literacy themes found in UNESCO’s AI competency frameworks for students and teachers, including safe use, ethical judgement, human agency, critical thinking and understanding how AI systems work.

This makes Glytch useful as a practical school workshop tool: it turns those themes into a visible, discussion-based activity where staff and students can see what needs checking before AI-supported work is trusted.

## What Glytch teaches

Glytch teaches AI capability, not AI products.

That means it is not tied to ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Copilot or any single AI company.

It gives schools a shared language for responsible AI use:

Prompt → Tool → Check → Evidence → Review → Controlled work

## Why it is suitable for schools

Glytch is designed for safe workshops:

- simulated outputs
- no real emails sent
- no files changed
- no real-world actions taken
- human review built into the learning model
- safe discussion of AI limits and risks

It helps students and staff understand that AI can be useful and still need checking.

## Recommended use

Glytch can support:

- staff CPD
- student AI literacy lessons
- sixth form study skills
- digital strategy conversations
- governor or SLT briefings
- careers and employability sessions
- responsible AI discussions

## Safe-use position

Schools should not enter personal pupil data or sensitive school data into the demo.

Glytch does not replace school policy, safeguarding judgement, data protection practice or professional review.

Schools should follow their own policies and relevant safeguarding, data protection and AI guidance.

## What success looks like

After a Glytch session, people should be able to explain:

- the difference between asking AI a question and using AI in a checked process
- why AI outputs should not be trusted automatically
- what needs human review
- why evidence matters
- when simple AI help is enough
- when AI should not be used

## Suggested leader message

Glytch helps us build practical AI literacy safely. It shows staff and students how AI can move from simple answers into structured, checked and reviewable work. The aim is not to replace judgement, but to help people understand where judgement, evidence and human review still matter.

## One-line summary

A safe way for schools to teach AI judgement, not just AI prompts.
